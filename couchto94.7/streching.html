Stretching Exercises

Counter the repetitive stress of cycling with these targeted routines. Daily stretching enhances flexibility, improves range of motion, reduces injury risk, and alleviates stiffness.

Protect Your Body

Perform these exercises 2–3 times per week (10–15 min) to keep your foundation strong. Focus on mobility for your hips and strength for your core to prevent knee and lower back pain.

Pre-Ride Warm-Up Stretches

Quick dynamic moves to wake up the body before riding or spin class. Do these for 3–5 minutes — no long holds.

Neck Rolls

Gently circle your head to loosen the neck and upper traps — relieves tension from aero position.

5–8 slow circles each direction + 5 side-to-side tilts per side

Cross-Body Arm Pull

Pull one arm across your chest to open the shoulders — great before gripping handlebars.

Hold 15–20s per side | 2 reps each side

Shoulder Rolls

Roll shoulders forward and backward to mobilize upper back and release tension.

10 forward + 10 backward (smooth, big circles)

Overhead Finger Interlace Stretch

Interlace fingers, press palms up overhead — opens chest and shoulders.

Hold 15–20s | 3 reps (breathe deeply)

Post-Ride / Recovery Stretches

Hold these longer to release tightness after rides. Do 2–3× per week for best results.

Hip Flexor Lunge

Relieves hip tightness and improves posture on the bike. Essential for preventing lower back strain during long rides.

Hold 30–60s per side | 2–3 Sets

Glute Bridge

Activates the posterior chain. Strong glutes take the pressure off your knees and lower back during heavy climbing.

3 Sets of 12–15 Reps

Cat-Cow Pose

Improves spinal mobility. Perfect for relieving neck and back stiffness after being tucked in an aero position.

10–15 Slow Breaths

Pigeon Pose

Deeply opens the hips and glutes. This is the "gold standard" stretch for preventing IT Band Syndrome.

Hold 60s per side

IT Band Foam Roll

Releases myofascial tightness along the outer thigh. Use post-ride to maintain muscle suppleness.

1–2 Mins per side

Pro Tip

Consistency beats intensity. Ten minutes of mobility work three times a week is significantly more effective than a one-hour session once a month. Stay supple to stay fast!
