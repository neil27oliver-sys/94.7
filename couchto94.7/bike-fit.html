Bike Fit Optimization

Maximum Power. Minimum Pain.

1. Frame Size

Before you tweak, ensure the foundation is right. A frame that is too large will cause lower back pain; too small will cramp your lungs.

Quick Check: You should have 1-2 inches of clearance over the top tube when standing over the bike.

2. Saddle Height

Using the LeMond Method for a powerful stroke:

Inseam (cm) × 0.883 = Height

Measure from the center of the Bottom Bracket (the crank bolt) to the very top of the saddle. When pedaling, your knee should have a slight bend (25-35°) at the bottom.

3. Saddle Fore/Aft (KOPS)

The Knee Over Pedal Spindle rule ensures your power is driving directly into the drivetrain.

With cranks horizontal (3 o'clock), a plumb line dropped from the bony bump below your kneecap should bisect the pedal axle perfectly.

4. Reach & Drop

If your hands go numb, your reach is likely too long. You want a 90° angle between your torso and your upper arms when resting on the hoods.

Drop: Racing position is lower, but for the 94.7, comfort is speed. Keep bars 0-5cm below saddle height for endurance.

5. Cleat Alignment

Position the cleats so the ball of your foot is directly over the pedal spindle. Angle them so your natural "heel-in" or "heel-out" gait is respected to save your ACLs.

Pro Tip for the 94.7:

Make adjustments in 3mm increments. A 1cm jump in saddle height is enough to cause an Achilles injury overnight. Small tweaks, big results!
